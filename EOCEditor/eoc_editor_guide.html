EOC Editor — Staff Guide

What you post here appears on the public emergency page within about one minute. Write everything as if a resident is reading it — because they are.

1 · Signing in

  1. Open the editor map and select Sign in with ArcGIS Online.
  2. Use your CoFOR ArcGIS account. You must be in the CoFOR EOC Editors group — if sign-in works but layers don't load, contact GIS to be added.

2 · Posting a new incident

  1. In the editor panel, choose Emergency Incidents and click the map at the incident location. One point per incident.
  2. Fill the form. Every field with a dropdown is a pick-list — you never type codes:
FieldWhat to enter
Incident Name The headline residents see. “Boil Water Notice — Pressure Zone 2”, not “BWN PZ2”.
Incident Type Pick the closest match (Flooding, Fire, Water System, Road…).
Severity Level Sets the color of the whole public page — see the table below.
Status Active for a live event, Scheduled for planned work, Monitoring for developing situations.
Public Description What happened, 1–3 plain sentences. No radio codes or jargon.
Protective Action / Instructions The most important field. Tell residents exactly what to do: “Boil tap water for 2 minutes before drinking or cooking until further notice.”
Location Description Where, in words: “Ammann Rd at Cibolo Creek crossing.”
Start / Estimated End When it takes effect; honest estimate for the end, or leave the end blank if unknown.
Issuing Department / Contact / Link Your department, the public phone line (non-emergency 830-249-8645), and a link to the full notice if one exists.
  1. If the incident covers an area (boil zone, outage area), sketch it on Affected Areas with the same name, severity, and status.
  2. If roads are affected, trace them on Road Closures — set Closure Type, Road Name, and a Detour Description.
  3. Check your work: open the public page and confirm the banner color and wording within a minute or so.

3 · Choosing the severity

LevelUse it for
1 Advisory Planned road work, scheduled maintenance, minor issues with no service impact.
2 Watch Developing weather, flood watch on the creeks, situations under monitoring, lane closures.
3 Warning Boil water notice, impassable low water crossing, active road flooding, extended outage, nearby wildfire.
4 Emergency Evacuation, shelter-in-place, life-safety threat. The public page turns crimson and pulses.
Rule of thumb: Level 4 means a resident should stop what they're doing. If you're unsure between two levels during a live event, pick the higher one and adjust later.

4 · Updating an incident

Click the feature on the map, edit the fields, save. Never create a second point for the same event — update the original. The public page shows an “Updated” time automatically. Escalate or de-escalate by changing the Severity; the banner follows on the next refresh.

5 · Closing an incident

  1. Set Status = Resolved on the incident point and any related area or closure features.
  2. Everything disappears from the public page automatically. When nothing active remains, the banner returns to green All Clear.
Do not delete features. Resolved records are the city's incident history and are used for after-action review.

6 · If something looks wrong

ProblemFix
Signed in but can't edit / layers missing You're not in the CoFOR EOC Editors group, or your account role lacks editing — contact GIS.
Public page banner is the wrong color A forgotten feature is still Active or Scheduled somewhere — check all three layers for anything not Resolved.
Edit isn't showing on the public page Wait a full minute (it refreshes every 60 seconds), then hard- refresh your own browser. Still missing? Contact GIS.
Sign-in bounces with an error Usually a redirect/URL issue on GIS's side — report the exact address in your browser bar to GIS.

7 · Quick reference

  • Public page: Emergency Notification landing page (also linked from the city website)
  • One point per incident · plain language · Instructions field always filled for anything Level 2 and up
  • Resolve, don't delete
  • Quarterly test: post a “TEST — disregard” Advisory, confirm it appears publicly, then resolve it.
